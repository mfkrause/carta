Ordered lists may be numbered with letters and roman numerals as well as plain
digits. A run of lowercase letters makes an alphabetic list, and the first
letter sets where it starts:

c. gamma
d. delta
e. epsilon

A sequence of roman numerals makes a roman list, counting in the usual way:

i. first
ii. second
iii. third
iv. fourth

A lone `i` opens a roman list on its own, since that is its natural reading at
the head of a list:

i. the only entry

When a list reaches the letter `i` partway through, it stays alphabetic rather
than switching to roman numerals:

h. eight
i. nine
j. ten

The delimiter may be a trailing parenthesis instead of a period, or a pair of
parentheses surrounding the marker:

a) parenthesis on the right
b) and another

(a) wrapped on both sides
(b) and its neighbour

An uppercase letter followed by a single space and a period reads as ordinary
prose, so this stays a paragraph:

B. Franklin kept a tidy ledger.

Giving the marker two spaces of separation turns it into a list instead:

A.  the first heading
B.  the second heading

Numbered markers carry their own starting value, and a list nests inside an item
when its markers are indented under the content:

iii. the third movement

     a) the opening phrase
     b) the answering phrase
